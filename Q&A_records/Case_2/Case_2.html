Phase 2. Constraint Analysis

Below is a summary of the key constraints (as stated in the user query), plus the overarching logic for automatic vs. manual quarter‐body joints:

  1. Operations have dependencies and must follow their predecessor in correct sequence.
  2. “S40_00001_Jig in” and “S40_01001_Set up working environment” must be the first two operations (only once overall), and “S40_00002_Jig out” must be the last operation (once overall).
  3. Either “S40_04012_Deburring int, positioning, attach them automatic” or “S40_04013_Deburring int, positioning, attach them manual” is the final operation for one 1/4 fuselage section, but each has required prerequisites.
  4. Automatic joint operations can only be done one at a time (strictly in series for each quarter‐body). Manual joint operations can be done in series or parallel, up to two parallel operations at a time, provided they are the same operation.
  5. Manual and automatic operations cannot run in parallel at all.
  6. Only the same manual operation can be parallelized; different manual operations cannot be performed simultaneously.
  7. After finishing “S40_04014_Deinstall LFT and rails” (auto path) or “S40_04013_Deburring int, positioning, attach them manual” (manual path), the next two steps must be “S40_02002_Cleanup and add sealant” then “S40_02003_Inspection.”
  8. If multiple 1/4 bodies are manually joined (in series or parallel), “S40_02002_Cleanup and add sealant” and “S40_02003_Inspection” are only executed once at the end of that manual batch.
  9. For multiple 1/4 bodies automatically jointed in series, only one “S40_02001_Set in position Rails and LFT” is needed at the start of that automatic run, and one “S40_04014_Deinstall LFT and rails” at the end.

Automatic vs. Manual Joint Logic for Four 1/4 Fuselage Sections in This Plan:

• Two of the quarter fuselage sections will be joined manually (in parallel, since we can do two at a time and they are the same final operation).
